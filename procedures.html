Joint Replacement

Hip & Knee Arthroplasty

Total Hip Replacement

Hip replacement surgery (total hip arthroplasty) involves removing the damaged ball-and-socket joint of the hip and replacing it with an artificial implant. This procedure relieves pain, improves mobility, and restores quality of life for patients suffering from hip osteoarthritis, avascular necrosis, or other degenerative hip conditions. Mr Bairstow performs hip replacement surgery at Mount Private Hospital using modern implant systems designed to maximise longevity and function.

What to Expect

  • Procedure typically takes 1.5–2 hours under general or spinal anaesthetic
  • Hospital stay usually 2–4 days
  • Gradual return to normal activities over 6–12 weeks
  • Most patients experience significant pain relief and improved mobility

Total Knee Replacement

Total knee arthroplasty involves replacing the damaged surfaces of the knee joint with metal and polyethylene implants. It is the most effective long-term treatment for advanced knee osteoarthritis. Mr Bairstow has extensive experience in primary total knee replacement and uses a meticulous approach to surgical technique, implant selection, and postoperative rehabilitation.

What to Expect

  • Procedure typically takes 1.5–2 hours
  • Hospital stay usually 3–5 days
  • Structured physiotherapy programme begins within 24 hours
  • Most patients return to full activity within 6 months

Partial (Unicompartmental) Knee Replacement

When osteoarthritis is confined to only one compartment of the knee (usually the medial or inner compartment), a partial knee replacement may be an option. This procedure replaces only the diseased portion of the knee, preserving healthy bone, cartilage, and ligaments. Recovery is generally faster than total knee replacement, with many patients experiencing a more natural feeling knee.

What to Expect

  • Procedure typically takes 1–1.5 hours
  • Shorter hospital stay than total knee replacement
  • Faster recovery — many patients walk unaided within days
  • Suitable for selected patients with isolated compartment disease
Sports & Knee Surgery

Sports Knee Surgery

ACL Reconstruction

Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) rupture is one of the most significant sports knee injuries. Complete ACL tears rarely heal on their own and typically require surgical reconstruction to restore stability and enable return to sport. Mr Bairstow performs ACL reconstruction using hamstring or patellar tendon grafts, with a focus on getting athletes back to full activity safely.

What to Expect

  • Arthroscopic procedure under general anaesthetic
  • Day surgery or one-night hospital stay
  • Structured rehabilitation programme of 9–12 months
  • High rates of return to sport with appropriate physiotherapy

Knee Arthroscopy

Knee arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat a range of knee conditions. Using small incisions and a tiny camera (arthroscope), Mr Bairstow can visualise the inside of the knee joint and perform surgical repair of the meniscus, cartilage, and other structures with minimal disruption to surrounding tissue.

Common Indications

  • Meniscal tears (partial meniscectomy or repair)
  • Cartilage lesions
  • Loose bodies in the knee joint
  • Synovitis and inflammatory joint disease

What to Expect

  • Typically performed as day surgery
  • Most patients return to light activities within 1–2 weeks
  • Sports return varies by procedure performed
Special Interest

Preoperative Planning & Simulation

Mr Bairstow has a particular interest in preoperative planning for lower limb joint replacements. Using advanced templating and simulation software, he carefully plans each joint replacement to optimise implant sizing, positioning, and lower limb alignment before entering the operating theatre. This detailed preparation minimises intraoperative surprises, reduces operative time, and contributes to improved long-term outcomes for patients.

Benefits of Detailed Preoperative Planning

Accurate sizing
Precise implant sizing and selection before surgery
Optimal positioning
Optimised component positioning and alignment
Leg length
Reduced risk of leg length discrepancy
Longevity
Improved implant longevity and function
Efficiency
Greater surgical confidence and efficiency
