Your Visit

Everything you need to know about your initial consultation and surgical journey with Mr Bairstow

Step 1

Your Initial Consultation

Your initial consultation with Mr Bairstow typically lasts 30–45 minutes. He will take a thorough history, review your imaging, perform a clinical examination, and discuss all available treatment options — both surgical and non-surgical — with you.

There is no obligation to proceed with surgery. Many patients benefit from optimising conservative management before considering an operation.

Important: A current GP or specialist referral is required for all appointments. Mr Bairstow does not accept self-referrals.

Step 2

What to Bring to Your Appointment

Please bring the following to your initial consultation:

Referral Letter

Your current GP or specialist referral letter. This is required for all appointments and for Medicare claims.

Health Insurance Details

Your Medicare card and private health insurance card. Please also bring any relevant fund details or pre-authorisation numbers.

Imaging

All relevant imaging — X-rays, MRI, and CT scans — on disc or as digital copies. Weight-bearing X-rays are preferred for joint conditions.

Medications List

A current list of all medications including supplements, anticoagulants, immunosuppressants, and over-the-counter medicines.

Previous Records

Any previous surgical records, letters, or specialist reports relevant to the condition you are being referred for.

Support Person

You are welcome to bring a family member or carer. Having a support person can be helpful, particularly if surgery is being discussed.

Patient Fees

Fees & Health Insurance

Mr Bairstow charges fees for his services as a private specialist. Out-of-pocket costs will vary depending on the procedure, your private health insurance cover, and individual circumstances.

We recommend contacting your private health insurer prior to your consultation to understand your cover for specialist consultations and, where relevant, for hospital admission. Our rooms can provide item numbers to assist with this enquiry.

Medicare Rebate

With a valid GP or specialist referral, you are entitled to a Medicare rebate for specialist consultations. There may be an out-of-pocket gap above the rebate. Contact our rooms for current fees.

Private Health Insurance

Private health insurance covers hospital admission and theatre costs. The extent of cover varies by fund and level of cover. We recommend contacting your insurer in advance to check your entitlements for any planned procedure.

Uninsured Patients

Patients without private health insurance are welcome. Full out-of-pocket fees will apply for both consultation and surgery. Please discuss costs with our rooms prior to your appointment so there are no surprises.

For all fee enquiries, please contact our rooms directly on (08) 9271 1552.

Your Patient Journey

01

Referral

Your GP refers you to Mr Bairstow with a referral letter and relevant imaging. Our rooms will contact you to arrange an appointment.

02

Consultation

Mr Bairstow reviews your history, imaging, and performs a clinical examination. All treatment options — surgical and non-surgical — are discussed with you.

03

Pre-operative Preparation

If surgery is planned, pre-operative assessments, planning, and hospital booking are arranged. You will receive information about what to expect in hospital.

04

Recovery & Follow-up

Post-operative care is coordinated between Mr Bairstow's rooms, physiotherapy, and your GP. Follow-up appointments are arranged to monitor your progress.

Before Your Surgery

  • You will receive a pre-admission call from Mount Private Hospital
  • Fasting instructions will be provided (typically nil by mouth from midnight)
  • Pre-operative blood tests and anaesthetic assessment may be arranged
  • Arrange transport home and support for the first few days post-operatively
  • Optimise your health: maintain a healthy weight, stop smoking, manage diabetes and other comorbidities prior to surgery
