About Mr Brett Bairstow

  • BMedSc (Hons) Bachelor of Medical Science with Honours

An Established Local Orthopaedic Surgeon

Mr Brett Bairstow is an established, local orthopaedic surgeon who has been serving the Perth community for over two decades. He completed his undergraduate studies at the University of Western Australia in 1993, graduating with a Bachelor of Medical Science with Honours and a Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery.

Specialist Training

Following his undergraduate studies, Mr Bairstow commenced specialist orthopaedic and trauma training in the Western Australian Royal Australasian College of Surgeons (RACS) and Australian Orthopaedic Association (AOA) training program. He achieved Fellowship of the Royal Australasian College of Surgeons (FRACS) in 2002 — a significant milestone representing the highest standard of surgical excellence in Australasia.

International Fellowship Training

Mr Bairstow completed additional post-FRACS fellowship training in arthroplasty and sports surgery at SportsMed, South Australia — one of Australia's premier orthopaedic sports medicine institutions. He further refined his arthroplasty skills at the Royal Bournemouth Hospital in the United Kingdom, gaining exposure to advanced lower limb joint replacement techniques.

This international and national fellowship training underpins Mr Bairstow's expertise in delivering world-class joint replacement outcomes for his patients.

Areas of Subspecialty

Mr Bairstow sub-specialises in:

  • Hip and knee replacement (arthroplasty)
  • Knee arthroscopic and sports surgery
  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) reconstruction
  • Preoperative planning including simulation of lower limb joint replacements

A particular area of interest for Mr Bairstow is in preoperative planning — including detailed simulation of lower limb joint replacements. This meticulous approach to surgical preparation contributes to better implant positioning, improved alignment, and superior long-term outcomes for patients.

Hospital Affiliations

Mr Bairstow's main operating list is at Mount Private Hospital in Perth — Western Australia's leading private provider of cardiac and orthopaedic surgery, with ICU facilities to support complex cases.

Consulting Locations

Mr Bairstow consults at Boorloo Specialist Centre Orthopaedic Clinic, Unit 2, 29 Walters Drive, Osborne Park WA 6017. He also visits the Northam Family Practice approximately every 6 weeks for the benefit of regional patients. A GP referral is required.
