About Your Surgery

What to expect if surgery is recommended.

Not necessarily. At your consultation, Mr Bairstow will discuss all treatment options — surgical and non-surgical. Many patients can be managed with physiotherapy, injections, or activity modification before considering surgery. Mr Bairstow will provide an honest recommendation based on your clinical findings and goals.

Mr Bairstow operates primarily at Mount Private Hospital, Perth. Please discuss your private health insurance cover with our rooms prior to booking surgery to ensure you understand your expected costs.

Yes. If surgery is planned, you will have a separate pre-operative assessment with an anaesthetist, usually arranged through the hospital's pre-admission service. This may be done in person or over the phone depending on your medical history.

Hospital stay depends on the procedure:

  • Knee arthroscopy and ACL reconstruction: typically day case or one night
  • Partial knee replacement: approximately 2–3 days
  • Total knee replacement: approximately 3–5 days
  • Total hip replacement: approximately 2–4 days

Mr Bairstow will advise you specifically based on your procedure and individual circumstances.

Recovery & Rehabilitation

What to expect during recovery after surgery.

This depends on the procedure and which leg is operated on. Generally, driving is not permitted for 6 weeks after major joint replacement (hip or knee). For arthroscopic procedures, return to driving may be earlier. Mr Bairstow will advise you specifically at your post-operative appointment.

Return to work timelines vary by procedure and occupation. Office-based workers typically return sooner than those with physically demanding jobs. As a general guide:

  • Knee arthroscopy: 1–2 weeks for sedentary work
  • ACL reconstruction: 2–4 weeks for sedentary work; longer for manual work
  • Joint replacement: 4–8 weeks for sedentary work; longer for physical roles

Discuss your specific situation and work requirements with Mr Bairstow.

Yes. Physiotherapy is an essential part of recovery for most orthopaedic procedures. Mr Bairstow will provide a referral and rehabilitation guidelines after your surgery. The duration and intensity of physiotherapy will depend on the procedure performed.

Yes. After joint replacement surgery, dental procedures that may cause bacteraemia (such as extractions, scale and clean, or root canal treatment) should be discussed with both your dentist and Mr Bairstow. Antibiotic prophylaxis may be recommended in certain circumstances to reduce the very small risk of joint infection. Please raise this with our rooms if you need dental treatment after your surgery.

Fees & Costs

Information about consultation and surgical fees.

Medicare provides a rebate for specialist consultations when you have a valid GP or specialist referral. There may be an out-of-pocket cost above the Medicare schedule fee. Please contact our rooms on (08) 9271 1552 for current consultation fees.

Private health insurance cover depends on your insurer, level of cover, and the specific procedure. We strongly recommend contacting your insurer in advance. Our rooms can provide item numbers to help you check your entitlements before you commit to surgery.

Uninsured patients are welcome. Please discuss costs with our rooms prior to your appointment, as full out-of-pocket fees will apply for both consultation and surgery. We aim to be transparent about expected costs so there are no surprises.
