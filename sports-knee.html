Sports Knee Surgery

Expert care for knee sports injuries in Perth — ACL, meniscus, cartilage and more

Sports knee injuries can sideline athletes for months — or permanently, without proper treatment. Mr Brett Bairstow's subspecialty includes knee arthroscopic and sports surgery, with a focus on restoring stability, function, and confidence for patients to return to the activities they love.

Sports Knee Conditions We Treat

ACL Rupture

Complete tears of the anterior cruciate ligament — the most common serious sports knee injury, often requiring surgical reconstruction in active patients.

Meniscal Tear

Tears of the medial or lateral meniscus causing pain, swelling, clicking, or locking. Treatment ranges from conservative management to arthroscopic repair or meniscectomy.

Patella Dislocation

Dislocation of the kneecap, most common in young active patients. Recurrent instability may require surgical stabilisation.

Cartilage Injury

Articular cartilage lesions from sporting impact or overuse. Arthroscopic assessment allows accurate diagnosis and targeted treatment.

Knee Ligament Injury

Tears of the collateral or posterior cruciate ligaments, often occurring with other knee injuries. Management is guided by severity and functional impact.

Sporting Knee Pain

Unexplained knee pain affecting sporting performance. Mr Bairstow will investigate the underlying cause and guide appropriate treatment.

Return to Sport — Rehabilitation Matters

Returning to sport after knee surgery requires not just a technically successful operation, but a structured, evidence-based rehabilitation programme. Mr Bairstow works closely with physiotherapists to provide personalised rehabilitation protocols tailored to each patient's sport, fitness level, and recovery progress.

Return-to-sport criteria are assessed objectively — ensuring patients are functionally ready, not simply a set number of weeks post-surgery.
